Understanding Anagrams in JavaScript

An anagram is a word or phrase that is formed by rearranging the letters of a different word or phrase, typically using all the original letters exactly once. For example, the word "listen" can be rearranged to form the word "silent."

Anagrams are often used in word games and puzzles. They can be a fun way to challenge your mind and improve your vocabulary. For instance, the words "cinema" and "iceman" are also anagrams of each other.

How Anagrams Work

To determine whether two words are anagrams, you can follow these steps:

  • Convert both words to lowercase to ensure the comparison is case-insensitive.
  • Remove any spaces or punctuation (if you're comparing phrases).
  • Sort the letters of both words alphabetically.
  • Compare the sorted versions of the words. If they match, the words are anagrams.

Examples

Here are some examples of anagrams:

  • “evil” and “vile”
  • “fluster” and “restful”
  • “rat” and “tar”

JavaScript Implementation

Below is an example of JavaScript code that checks if two words are anagrams: 


function areAnagrams(word1, word2) {
    let cleanedWord1 = word1.toLowerCase().split('').sort().join('');
    let cleanedWord2 = word2.toLowerCase().split('').sort().join('');
    return cleanedWord1 === cleanedWord2;
}
